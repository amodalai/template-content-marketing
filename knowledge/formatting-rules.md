# Formatting Rules

## Punctuation

- **Avoid hyphen-based sentence structures.** Instead of using em dashes or hyphens for parenthetical information, use commas, parentheses, or break into separate sentences.
  - ❌ "The report shows strong growth—especially in Q3."
  - ❌ "The report shows strong growth - especially in Q3."
  - ✅ "The report shows strong growth, especially in Q3."
  - ✅ "The report shows strong growth (especially in Q3)."
  - ✅ "The report shows strong growth. Q3 was particularly strong."

## Word Choice

- **Use strong verbs over weak verb + adverb combinations.** Replace generic verbs modified by adverbs with more specific, powerful verbs.
  - ❌ "quickly run" → ✅ "sprint" or "dash"
  - ❌ "said loudly" → ✅ "shouted" or "declared" 
  - ❌ "carefully examine" → ✅ "scrutinize" or "analyze"
  - ❌ "slowly walk" → ✅ "stroll" or "amble"
  - ❌ "completely destroy" → ✅ "obliterate" or "demolish"

## Emoji Usage

- **Use emojis sparingly.** Technical content should be clear and professional first, with minimal decorative elements.
- **Limit to 1 emoji per tweet/post maximum** - only when it genuinely adds value or clarity
- **Avoid emojis in:**
  - Technical documentation or tutorials
  - Professional reports and analysis
  - Formal business communications
  - Most written content where text alone suffices
- **Consider emojis only for:**
  - Major announcements or milestones
  - Expressing genuine enthusiasm for significant achievements
  - Drawing attention to urgent/important information
- **When used, place strategically** - typically at the end of a statement for emphasis, not as decoration

## Guidelines

- Use commas for brief parenthetical information
- Use parentheses for additional context or clarification
- Break complex thoughts into separate sentences for clarity
- For range expressions, regular hyphens are still appropriate (e.g., "pages 1-10", "2020-2022")
- For compound adjectives, hyphens remain correct (e.g., "well-known author", "state-of-the-art system")
- Emojis should enhance the technical message, not replace clear communication
